Graphical Sequences Verification

Checking if Degree Sequences Can Form Simple Graphs

Given Sequences

Sequence 1: (7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 3, 2)
Sequence 2: (6, 6, 5, 4, 3, 3, 1)

🔹 Graphical Sequence

A sequence of non-negative integers is graphical if there exists a simple graph with vertices having exactly those degrees. We'll verify using two methods:

  • Erdős–Gallai Theorem: Provides necessary and sufficient conditions
  • Havel-Hakimi Algorithm: Constructive method that reduces the problem step by step

Sequence 1: (7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 3, 2)

1Basic Checks

Sum of degrees: 7 + 6 + 5 + 4 + 3 + 3 + 2 = 30

Is sum even? Yes ✓ (necessary condition for any graph)

Number of vertices: n = 7

Maximum possible degree: n - 1 = 6

❌ Problem: We have a vertex of degree 7, but maximum possible degree is 6!
❌ SEQUENCE 1 IS NOT GRAPHICAL
Fails basic degree bound check

1Initial Check

(7, 6, 5, 4, 3, 3, 2)

We immediately see that vertex degree 7 > n - 1 = 6, which is impossible in a simple graph with 7 vertices.

❌ Termination: Cannot connect a vertex to 7 others when there are only 6 other vertices!
❌ SEQUENCE 1 IS NOT GRAPHICAL
Degree exceeds vertex limit

Sequence 2: (6, 6, 5, 4, 3, 3, 1)

1Basic Checks

Sorted sequence: (6, 6, 5, 4, 3, 3, 1) ✓ Already in non-increasing order

Sum of degrees: 6 + 6 + 5 + 4 + 3 + 3 + 1 = 28

Is sum even? Yes ✓

Maximum degree ≤ n-1? 6 ≤ 6 ✓

2Erdős–Gallai Inequality

∑(i=1 to k) d_i ≤ k(k-1) + ∑(i=k+1 to n) min(d_i, k)

Must hold for all k = 1, 2, ..., n

k Left Side: ∑d_i k(k-1) ∑min(d_i, k) Right Side Check
1 6 0 6+5+4+3+3+1=22 22 6 ≤ 22 ✓
2 12 2 2+2+2+2+1=9 11 12 ≤ 11 ❌
❌ Fails at k = 2: 12 > 11
❌ SEQUENCE 2 IS NOT GRAPHICAL
Fails Erdős–Gallai inequality at k = 2

1Step 1

(6, 6, 5, 4, 3, 3, 1)

Remove the first element (6) and subtract 1 from the next 6 largest elements:

(6, 6-1, 5-1, 4-1, 3-1, 3-1, 1-1) = (5, 4, 3, 2, 2, 0)

2Step 2

(5, 4, 3, 2, 2, 0)

Remove the first element (5) and subtract 1 from the next 5 largest elements:

(5, 4-1, 3-1, 2-1, 2-1, 0-1) = (3, 2, 1, 1, -1)
❌ Negative degree! We get -1, which is impossible.
❌ SEQUENCE 2 IS NOT GRAPHICAL
Havel-Hakimi produces negative degree

Summary

📊Results

Sequence (7,6,5,4,3,3,2)
❌ NOT GRAPHICAL
Degree 7 > n-1 = 6
Sequence (6,6,5,4,3,3,1)
❌ NOT GRAPHICAL
Fails Erdős–Gallai & Havel-Hakimi

🔍Why These Sequences Fail

  • Sequence 1: Impossible to have degree 7 in a simple graph with only 7 vertices (max degree = 6)
  • Sequence 2: The high degrees of the first two vertices (both 6) require too many connections, leaving insufficient degree capacity for the remaining vertices
