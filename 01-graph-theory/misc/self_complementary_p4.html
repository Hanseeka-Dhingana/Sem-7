Non-isomorphic self-complementary graphs on 4 vertices

On 4 vertices the only (up to isomorphism) self-complementary simple graph is the path P₄ (3 edges). Below: the graph G and its complement Ĝ, and an explicit isomorphism showing they are the same up to relabeling.

G = P4
Edges: {1-2, 2-3, 3-4}
Ĝ = complement(G)
Edges: {1-3, 1-4, 2-4}
Isomorphism demonstrating G ≅ Ĝ
Relabeling f: (1↦1, 2↦4, 3↦2, 4↦3)
Apply f to Ĝ's vertices: edges {1-3,1-4,2-4} become {1-2,2-3,3-4} which are exactly G's edges. Hence Ĝ ≅ G, so P₄ is self-complementary.
